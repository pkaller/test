03 · How RFQ Works

Structured dealer competition.

Rather than placing an order into the public market — where your intent is visible and prices move against you — RFQ sends a private, simultaneous request to multiple dealers. They compete without knowing each other's quotes, driving prices toward fair value.

Step 01

Private Request

A simultaneous RFQ is sent privately to all relevant market makers — specifying token, strike, expiry, size, and direction. No public order book signal.

Step 02

Blind Competition

Each dealer has a short response window to submit their best price. They know they are competing but cannot see rival quotes — creating genuine price competition.

Step 03

Best Price Selected

All responses are evaluated and the best price is selected. For sell-to-open trades, the highest premium is chosen. The full quote set is retained as an audit record.

Step 04

Trade Confirmed

Execution is confirmed and a same-day post-trade report is issued to the client — strike, expiry, premium received, and portfolio impact.
