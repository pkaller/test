Options Trading

A Systematic Options Program Built for Foundations

Options are not speculation. In our hands, they are precision instruments for income generation and risk reduction — entirely consistent with foundation fiduciary duty.

Why Foundations Benefit from Options

Foundations face a structural challenge: generate at least 5% annually for distribution while preserving real purchasing power over a perpetual horizon. In a world of compressed equity returns and low bond yields, systematic options writing offers a proven, repeatable mechanism to enhance portfolio income without taking on undue directional risk.

Our call option writing program generates premium income — typically 3–6% annualized on notional value — by selling covered calls against existing equity positions. This income supplements dividends and interest, directly supporting distribution requirements without requiring asset liquidation.

Simultaneously, our protective put and collar programs provide catastrophic loss protection — crucial for foundations with perpetual mandates that cannot afford sequence-of-returns risk.

01

Covered Call Writing

We systematically sell call options against long equity positions held in the foundation portfolio. Each call is sold OTM (typically 5–10% above current price) with 30–45 days to expiration, generating consistent premium income while allowing meaningful equity participation.

Cash-Secured Puts

We sell put options on equities the foundation wishes to own at lower prices, collecting premium while waiting for attractive entry points. If assigned, we acquire quality assets at a discount. If not, premium income is retained.

Protective Collars

How We Trade

The RFQ Process: Institutional Best Execution

We execute all listed options trades via the Request for Quote (RFQ) mechanism — the institutional gold standard for complex options orders. This ensures competitive pricing, full documentation, and fiduciary-grade execution quality for every client trade.

1

Strategy Determination

Our options desk identifies the optimal strategy, strike, and expiration based on the client's current holdings, income objectives, and risk parameters defined in their Investment Policy Statement.

→
2

RFQ Submission

We submit a structured Request for Quote to multiple market makers simultaneously via electronic trading venues (CBOE, ISE, BOX, Nasdaq PHLX). The RFQ specifies the exact contract, size, and requested response time.

→
3

Quote Competition

Registered market makers compete to provide the best bid/ask. Competition among multiple dealers — typically 3–8 respondents — drives tighter spreads and better execution than hitting the open market. Quotes are time-stamped and logged.

→
4

Best Execution & Fill

We select the best quote (or negotiate further on large blocks), execute the trade, and generate a complete execution record — including all competing quotes — for client reporting and regulatory audit trail purposes.

→
5

Post-Trade Reporting

Every executed options trade is reflected in client-facing daily reports with full position details: underlying, strike, expiration, premium received/paid, delta, and P&L impact. Clients have 24/7 access to their options book via our portal.

Why RFQ Matters to Your Foundation

◇

Fiduciary Best Execution

Competing quotes create a documented best-execution record — essential for foundation trustees fulfilling their fiduciary duty under ERISA and state law.

◇

Tighter Spreads on Large Blocks

RFQ dramatically reduces the bid-ask spread on block options trades (100+ contracts), saving 5–15 basis points per round-trip versus retail execution.

◇

Full Audit Trail

Every quote, every respondent, every time-stamp is captured. This documentation is available to boards, auditors, and regulators on demand.

◇

Price Transparency

Clients can independently verify that the fill received was the best available in the market at the time of execution — eliminating hidden execution costs.
