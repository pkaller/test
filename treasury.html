Treasury Strategy: Options Overlay

Systematic yield generation for token treasuries via covered call overlays — how foundations can generate consistent premium income without taking on directional risk, executed through institutional RFQ.

Executive Summary

Most token treasuries hold large positions in their native token with no mechanism for generating yield. The assets sit idle, exposed to price volatility, with no income except discretionary sales that dilute the position or depress the market.

A systematic covered call overlay changes this. By selling short-dated call options — struck 30–50% above the current spot price — a treasury generates consistent premium income in every market cycle, regardless of whether prices rise, fall, or consolidate. Execution via Request for Quote ensures institutional-grade pricing on every trade, with a full audit record for governance.

01 — The Underutilized Treasury Problem

Token foundations and protocol treasuries routinely hold hundreds of millions of dollars in their native token. These holdings exist for a purpose: funding development, grants, ecosystem growth. But between deployment decisions, the capital is idle — earning nothing and subject to full downside exposure.

  • Idle holdings generate no income while retaining full downside risk
  • Discretionary token sales create selling pressure and market perception problems
  • Stablecoin yields are modest and require converting the core asset
  • Options markets now have sufficient depth at block size to support systematic programs via RFQ

The covered call overlay addresses this directly: it monetizes the token's implied volatility premium without requiring the treasury to sell its underlying position.

02 — Strategy Design: Covered Call Overlay

The strategy sells short-dated out-of-the-money call options against a portion of the treasury's token holdings. The key parameters are:

  • Strike selection: 130–150% of current spot price — meaningfully above the market, limiting assignment risk
  • Tenor: 30–45 day options, capturing the maximum time decay per unit of time
  • Coverage: typically 5–15% of holdings per month, rolling continuously
  • Execution: simultaneous RFQ to all relevant market makers for best price

"A token trading at $100 with a 130% strike call selling at $4/month generates $48 in annual premium — a 48% yield on the notional covered — without requiring any token sales."

03 — Yield Generation Mechanics

Options premiums reflect the market's expectation of future volatility. Crypto assets typically carry significantly higher implied volatility than traditional assets — this translates directly into higher option premiums and therefore higher potential yield from a systematic selling program.

Three forces drive premium collection:

  • Time decay (theta): every day that passes without the option moving into the money, the option loses value — that decay accrues to the seller
  • Volatility premium: implied volatility in crypto options markets consistently exceeds realised volatility, creating a structural edge for systematic sellers
  • Repetition: rolling monthly programs compound the premium collection effect across 12 cycles per year

04 — Execution: RFQ-Based Price Discovery

Execution quality is the single biggest variable in an options income program. The difference between a well-executed and a poorly-executed block trade can represent 10–30% of the total premium collected. Mosaic executes every trade via Request for Quote — broadcasting simultaneously to all relevant market makers and selecting the best response.

  • No pre-trade signalling: order intent is not visible to the market before execution
  • Forced competition: 3–8 market makers quote simultaneously, driving prices toward fair value
  • Complete audit record: every competing quote is time-stamped and retained
  • Full coverage: even for smaller tokens, all relevant dealers are contacted — not just the most liquid

05 — Market Impact and Flow Dynamics

A concern sometimes raised about covered call programs is that systematic option selling creates one-sided flow that depresses implied volatility and ultimately reduces the yield. In practice, the dynamic is more constructive.

When a market maker buys a call option from the treasury, they delta-hedge by buying the underlying token. This creates incremental buying pressure in the spot market — partially offsetting any perception of selling pressure associated with the program. The overall flow effect tends to be two-sided and stabilising rather than directional.

For tokens where the foundation is a significant holder, a systematic options program can also signal confidence in the project's long-term trajectory — the act of selling calls at 130–150% of spot is an implicit statement that the team expects the price to remain below those levels over the near term.

06 — Implications and Trade-offs

No yield-generating strategy is without trade-offs. A covered call overlay is well-suited to foundations with specific characteristics — and less suited to others.

Benefits

  • Consistent premium income in all market conditions
  • No requirement to sell the underlying token
  • Partial downside buffer from accumulated premiums
  • Stabilising market impact from dealer delta-hedging
  • Full audit trail for governance and investment committees

Trade-offs

  • Capped upside: if the token rallies above the strike, the covered portion does not participate fully
  • Assignment risk: in a strong rally, called-away tokens must be repurchased to maintain position
  • Programme commitment: consistent execution is required to realise the compounding effect
  • Perception: some stakeholders may view option writing as speculative without understanding the mechanics

Conclusion

A systematic covered call overlay is one of the few mechanisms available to token foundations that generates consistent yield without requiring the sale of core treasury assets. The strategy exploits the structural volatility premium in crypto options markets, compounds through rolling monthly cycles, and produces a full governance audit trail as a by-product of RFQ execution.

For foundations holding significant native token positions, the question is not whether the strategy is appropriate — it is whether the execution infrastructure exists to implement it at institutional quality. That is what Mosaic provides.
